Who am I?

If you haven't already guessed, my name is Murdock. I am originally from Collingwood, Ontario, however I now live in downtown Toronto. I have always pushed myself to be the best at everything I can, and in the former half of my life, this largely took the form of freestlye skiing; some videos can be found here. However, for the last several years, most of my efforts have been focussed on cultivating my technical skills. More generally, I love solving difficult problems.

I am currently in my fourth and final year of the math and physics specialist program at the University of Toronto. Much of my studies are focused on numerical analysis, computer modelling, and machine learning. I am presently conducting research on topics in numerical analysis under the supervision of Professor James Bremer, and separately on machine learning under the supervision of Professor Nikolina Ilic. More information on my current and past projects can be found here. A closer look at my academic background can be found here.



What is next?

With respect to my education, graduate school is next. Have endured a highly abstract and conseputal undergraduate program, I am looking to transition my studies to a much more applied and computationally-oriented masters degree program. One area that particularly captivates me is generative design optimization, a realm where machine learning thrives in creating innovative solutions within specified parameters. This can be achieved through the use of generative adversarial networks (GANs), a model first introduced in 2014 by Ian J. Goodfellow. Companies like NVIDIA and Autodesk are at the forefront of these advancements, harnessing the power of artificial intelligence to reshape the landscape of design and engineering. I wish to utilize my time in graduate school to push the limits of what is possible.
