Tutorial 1 (Week 2)

We begin by introducing the following pieces of notation: \begin{align*} [f] = \begin{pmatrix} f(x_{1, k}) \\ f(x_{2, k}) \\ \vdots \\ f(x_{k, k}) \end{pmatrix} && \text{diag}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} v_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & v_2 & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \, & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & v_n \end{pmatrix} \end{align*} where the collection $\{x_{i, k}\}_{i=1}^k$ is the $k-$point Chebyshev quadrature constructed on an interval $(a, b)$ and $\vec{v}\in \mathbb{F}^n$ is a vector of length $n$.
